About

Jeju Island is a beautiful destination located just off the southern coast of South Korea, known for its unique volcanic landscapes and natural scenery. The island is home to Hallasan Mountain, the highest peak in the country, along with waterfalls, lava tubes, and scenic beaches. With its mix of nature, local culture, and great food, Jeju has become one of the most popular getaway spots in Korea. This site shares some of the best ways to experience Jeju, from places to eat to places to explore, curated from hours of online research.

Things To Do

  1. Mount Hallasan - Standing at 1,950m above sea level, Mount Hallasan is South Korea's tallest mountatin and takes around 6-8 hours to fully hike.
  2. Seongsan Ilchulbong - Also known as Sunrise Peak, Seongsan Ilchulbong is a volcano on the eastern end of the island and is a beautiful spot to catch the sunrise over the ocean.
  3. Jeju Haenyeo Museum - Haenyeo are female divers in Jeju, some aged in their 80s, who go diving 10m under the ocean to gather shellfish. This museum showcases the history of Jeju's deep haenyeo culture, with everything donated by the haenyeos themselves.
  4. Camellia Hill - Visit the Camellia Hill to see a breathtaking view of over 6,000 camellia trees and 500 different species of wildflowers.
  5. Snoopy Garden - Nothing more needs to be said...
  6. Gamttanam - Jeju tangerines are the signature specialty of the island, so if you're visiting, you can't not go try them. Gamttanam is a popular tangerine field and cafe where guests can pick their own tangerines from the field and get a cup of freshly squeezed juice afterwards.

Foods You Can't Miss

When in Jeju, its incredibly fresh seafood and black pork are a must-try according to both locals and travelers. From grilled black pork belly to comforting seafood stews and fresh abalone dishes, Jeju's specialties reflect the island's coastal location and unique culture.
